ABOUT THE COLLECTION

The cosmetics catalogue exists as an archive of open source beauty product advertising of the 20th and 21st centuries. While many view makeup as a tool for self expression to highlight their own unique look and style, the media has historically created a visual language for beauty that is the opposite. Often defining beauty in terms of the feminine, western, cis-gender “woman”, cosmetics advertising has (and continues to) target a selective audience that identifies with these constricting values.

Repetition and immobility can not only be found within the values and portrayal of the industry, but extend to the recurring content and format of the ads themselves. Throughout my research I have found that the majority of cosmetics/beauty advertisements can be placed into one of the following tropes: the flower, the couture, the nude, and the traveler.
